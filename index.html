Gregorio Y Zara

A pioneering Filipino scientist and inventor, best known for creating the first videophone and advancing renewable energy. His innovations in engineering and dedication to progress earned him the title of National Scientist. Zara’s legacy continues to inspire generations, marking him as a visionary in Philippine science and technology.

Timeline

Early Life and Education

1902 Gregorio Y. Zara is born on March 8 in Lipa, Batangas, Philippines.

1918 Graduates with honors from Batangas High School, showing early academic promise.

1922-1926 Earns a Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering from the University of the Philippines (UP) and then a scholarship to the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).

1926-1930 Completes a degree in Aeronautical Engineering from MIT, followed by further studies at the University of Michigan, where he graduates with distinction.

Scientific Contributions and Inventions

1930s Begins work as a researcher and scientist back in the Philippines, working on various government projects.

1939 Patents the first videophone (photo phone) in the Philippines, demonstrating his innovation in telecommunications.

1946 Develops an alcohol-powered engine, advancing renewable energy applications in the Philippines.

1955 Invents a solar-powered water heater, one of the earliest examples of solar technology in the country.

Academic and Government Service

1953-1966 Serves as a professor and later dean at the University of the Philippines, contributing to the education and mentorship of future Filipino scientists and engineers.

1960s Appointed to various government and advisory roles, including in the Philippine Department of Science and Technology, where he promotes scientific research and development.

Awards and Recognition

1966 Receives the "National Scientist Award" from the Philippine government, acknowledging his outstanding contributions to science and technology.

Late 1960s-1970s Continues to be recognized with various honors and awards for his innovations and influence in Philippine science.

Legacy and Passing

1978 Gregorio Zara passes away on October 15, leaving a legacy of scientific achievement and dedication to national progress.

Posthumous Honors His life’s work continues to inspire future generations, with tributes, publications, and institutions honoring his name and contributions to science and technology in the Philippines.
